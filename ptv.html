Places To Vizit

There are many attractions in Belarus, but we will tell you only about three of them.

Mirskiy Castle

From the very beginning of its existence, Mir Castle is surrounded by legends and secrets. For example, the exact
date of the start of construction of the castle complex is still not known for certain. There is only an assumption that it began no earlier than 1522 by order of Yuri Ilinich, the owner of these places at that time.

Also, not everything is clear with the goal of building the Mir Castle. There is an opinion that the fortress was
erected only for reasons of prestige, since the World at that time was a fairly calm place.

But at the same time, the thickness of the castle walls, in the lower part of which loopholes are cut, reaches 2-3
meters. In addition, the north and west walls had battle galleries. All this indicates a good defensive ability of the fortress in case of hostilities.

And nowadays, Mir Castle is surrounded by many legends that create an atmosphere of mystery and, like a
magnet, attract tourists.

Brest Fortress

Brest Fortress: it was here that fierce battles took place in 41 years. Soviet troops escaped from the German. Now
on the walls of this building you can find inscriptions of a similar type: “There were five of us: Sedov, Grutov I., Bogolyubov, Mikhailov, Selivanov V. We took the first battle on 22.VI.1941. We’ll die, but we won’t leave! ”- it is written on the bricks of the outer wall near the Terespol Gate.

In the western part of the barracks, in one of the rooms, the following inscription was found: “There were three of
us, it was difficult for us, but we did not lose heart and die like heroes. July. 1941. "

Many tourists annually visit this tragic place, which played a small role during the war.

Khatyn

Khatyn: a village that does not exist ...

The name of the Belarusian village of Khatyn is known all over the world, although now it no longer exists, since
the life of this settlement tragically ended on March 22, 1943. The village was not abandoned by the inhabitants, but died along with people and will never rise from the ashes again.

The death of this and other Belarusian villages, the death of every fourth Belarusian during the Great Patriotic War
was dedicated to the Khatyn memorial complex opened in 1969.

The complex itself is an example of a harmonious fusion of arts, which has no equal in the world in terms of
impact on visitors. The embodiment of the spirit of deep sorrow and eternal memory, a symbol of greatness and tragic heroism - that is Khatyn.
